# Handoff Protocol: From Maestro to Researcher

## Overview
This document outlines the standardized process for Maestro to delegate research tasks to the Researcher mode. A clear, comprehensive handoff ensures that the Researcher has all necessary information to conduct effective research that meets project needs.

## Handoff Structure

### 1. Research Request Context
Maestro MUST provide the following contextual information:
- **Project Background**: Brief overview of the project and its current status
- **Previous Research**: Summary of any relevant research already conducted
- **Current Project Phase**: Where in the project lifecycle this research is needed
- **Key Stakeholders**: Who will be using the research findings
- **Related Documentation**: Links to relevant project documents

### 2. Research Objectives
Maestro MUST clearly articulate:
- **Primary Research Goal**: The main purpose of the research
- **Specific Research Questions**: Detailed questions the research should answer
- **Decisions to Inform**: What decisions will be made based on this research
- **Success Criteria**: How the quality and usefulness of research will be evaluated

### 3. Research Scope
Maestro MUST define:
- **Topics to Include**: Specific areas the research should cover
- **Topics to Exclude**: Areas explicitly out of scope
- **Depth vs. Breadth**: Whether to prioritize comprehensive coverage or deep dives
- **Technical Depth**: Level of technical detail required
- **Comparative Elements**: What comparisons should be included (if any)

### 4. Constraints and Parameters
Maestro MUST specify:
- **Timeline**: When research findings are needed
- **Resource Limitations**: Any constraints on research resources
- **Format Requirements**: Required format for research deliverables
- **Confidentiality Considerations**: Any privacy or confidentiality requirements
- **Bias Awareness**: Any potential biases to be mindful of

### 5. Preferred Research Methods
Maestro SHOULD indicate:
- **Suggested Methodologies**: Any preferred research approaches
- **Required Sources**: Sources that must be consulted
- **Credibility Requirements**: Standards for source evaluation
- **Primary vs. Secondary**: Balance between primary and secondary research
- **Quantitative vs. Qualitative**: Balance between quantitative and qualitative data

### 6. Deliverables
Maestro MUST specify:
- **Required Outputs**: What research products are needed
- **Format and Structure**: How findings should be organized and presented
- **Level of Detail**: How comprehensive the deliverables should be
- **Visualization Requirements**: Any specific data visualization needs
- **Recommendation Expectations**: Whether and what kind of recommendations are needed

### 7. Handoff Acknowledgment
The Researcher MUST acknowledge receipt of the handoff by:
- **Confirming Understanding**: Restating the research objectives and requirements
- **Clarifying Ambiguities**: Asking questions about any unclear aspects
- **Proposing Approach**: Outlining the planned research methodology
- **Setting Expectations**: Providing a timeline for research completion
- **Requesting Additional Information**: Identifying any missing information needed

## Best Practices for Maestro

### Providing Clear Objectives
- Be specific about what you need to learn
- Prioritize research questions by importance
- Connect research to specific decisions
- Distinguish between must-have and nice-to-have information
- Avoid overly broad or vague research requests

### Setting Appropriate Scope
- Be realistic about what can be researched in the available time
- Clearly define boundaries to prevent scope creep
- Provide guidance on depth vs. breadth priorities
- Specify any areas that should be explicitly excluded
- Consider breaking very large research needs into phases

### Communicating Context
- Provide sufficient background information
- Share relevant prior research to avoid duplication
- Explain how the research fits into the larger project
- Identify key stakeholders and their information needs
- Include links to relevant project documentation

### Specifying Deliverables
- Clearly describe the required outputs
- Provide templates or examples when possible
- Specify the level of detail needed
- Indicate whether recommendations are required
- Clarify any formatting or structural requirements

### Setting Realistic Timelines
- Allow adequate time for thorough research
- Consider the complexity of the research questions
- Account for potential challenges in data collection
- Include time for review and refinement
- Specify any interim check-ins or progress reports

## Response Expectations for Researcher

Upon receiving a research request, the Researcher MUST:

1. **Acknowledge Receipt**: Confirm that the research request has been received
2. **Verify Understanding**: Restate the key objectives and requirements
3. **Ask Clarifying Questions**: Identify and ask about any unclear aspects
4. **Propose Methodology**: Outline the planned research approach
5. **Confirm Timeline**: Provide an estimated completion timeline
6. **Request Missing Information**: Identify any additional information needed
7. **Suggest Refinements**: Offer constructive feedback on the research request
8. **Set Next Steps**: Establish when the next update will be provided

The Researcher should respond to the handoff within 24 hours or as specified in the project timeline.
